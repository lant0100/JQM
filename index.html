Step 1 of 8

Step 1: Be comfortable on your board!

Before getting into working on your trick it's important to make sure you are comfortable on your board. Get a good feel for your board! Practice riding around on your skateboard and work on your balance while you go too. Comfort and balance come along way when learning to perform am ollie!

Step 2 of 8

Step 2: Set-up your footing!

Try to set up your feet like the picture shown above! Front foot behind your front wheels closer to the centre of your board, and your back foot on the tail. The positioning of your front foot helps adjust the height of your ollie the further back it is the bigger jumps you can pull off but for now it's good practice with the positioning closer to the front of the board until you find yourself more comfortable!

Step 3 of 8

Step 3: Setting up your stance!

The next step is to prepare you stance for your jump! Practice your stance a few times before going ahead on your board. The stance should look like this, knees bent and body crouched, don't be crouched too far forward or backwards as it can effect your balance and the direction of your board. Try to keep your shoulders level with your feet!

Step 4 of 8

Step 4: The Jump!

To do this next step you need to make sure your stance is prepared from the last 2 steps (Step 2 and 3). If you are already driving you board use a small jump or slide your feet into position on your board! Next once your feet are in position you can perform the jump. Now you need to jump into the air, first on your front foot and then with your back. This lifts the front of your board first and sets you up for Step 5!

Step 5 of 8

Step 5: The "Pop"!

Next while performing your jump the jump with your back foot should also kick the back of your board down at the same time, this causes the board to bounce of the ground you should be able to hear the bounce thus the name "pop"! A good strong pop takes practice but is the power behind this trick! After jumping try to pop the board once you can feel your weight lifting off the board! This part of the technique is more complicated, if the pop isn't strong enough or if the board doesn't have enough air time before your pop the ollie becomes more difficult! Visualizing this movement and practice will help a lot!

Step 6 of 8

Step 6: Prep the Landing!

Now once the board gets the air from the "pop" (see Step 5) you're going to want to use your airtime to tuck your front foot in (see photo above) and slide it up your board, while at the same time bringing your back leg up towards your chest. This is to make sure you are levelling the board for your landing! Remember timing on this step, sliding too soon can affect this height of your ollie and sliding too late will make it harder to level your board for you landing!

Step 7 of 8

Step 7: Nail the Landing!

Now while finishing your landing prep (Step 6) you should be bringing both your knees to your chest while pushing the board forward to level it! Once your front foot has slid up near your front wheels (as seen in Step 2) you want to keep levelling the board and make sure you keep pressure on it! Then try to land both feet at the same time with both feet respectively close to each truck (wheel support) as possible. This helps prevent damage to the board. Make sure you bend your knees as well and absorb the shock on your landing!

Step 8 of 8

Step 8: Practice and Perfect!

Once you smoothly land after your jump ride it out and practice some more! This trick takes lots of practice and is a good building block to begin more advanced lessons! Keep trying and get the feel of your Ollie skills, go out and show off the new trick you have just picked up! Make sure whenever practicing or performing with a skateboard to wear the right gear (helmet)! Stay safe while learning or just showing off your new skills!
